# Sophia's Airpark Adventures
## Season 1 Episode Guide: "Learning to Look Before You Leap"

**Episodes:** 11  
**Runtime:** 10-12 minutes each  
**Target Age:** 3-6 years

---

## SERIES OVERVIEW

Sophia is a curious 6-year-old Filipina girl living in the best place in the world — an airpark! With her loyal dog Piper by her side, she explores lakes, hangars, runways, and everything in between. But her eagerness to discover everything often gets her into trouble. Each episode, Sophia learns important life lessons the best way — by making mistakes and figuring things out.

---

## MAIN CHARACTERS

**SOPHIA (6)** — Curious, energetic, heart bigger than her patience. Long dark hair, round face, big brown eyes. Quick to jump in, slow to look first.

**PIPER** — Sophia's scruffy tan mutt. Loyal, expressive ears, often mirrors Sophia's emotions. Sometimes gets into the same trouble she does.

**MOM (ANNA)** — Warm, organized Filipina mother. The anchor of the family. Helps Sophia process her feelings after adventures.

**DAD (BILL)** — Calm, handy pilot with a yellow Piper Cub. Asks questions instead of giving answers. Appears in workshop and flying scenes.

**GUS** — Retired pilot, 70s, white beard. Lives three hangars down. Tells rambling stories that somehow always contain exactly the wisdom Sophia needs.

**MAX** — Sophia's age, cautious, thinks everything through. Her opposite and balance.

**LILY** — Younger (4), looks up to Sophia, which makes Sophia want to be better.

## SEASON 1 EPISODE GUIDE

### Episode 1: "The New Hangar" ✈️ PILOT
> Sophia explores an old hangar she was told to stay away from. She gets stuck and scared before Dad finds her.

**Lesson:** Rules aren't just "no" — they exist for reasons you might not see yet.

**Airpark Elements:** Hangars, old airplane, Piper Cub, deer sighting

---

### Episode 2: "Piper's Big Escape"
> Sophia leaves the back gate open and Piper runs off. She searches the airpark frantically and finally finds him stuck in a muddy spot by the lake.

**Lesson:** Small careless moments can create big problems. Slow down on the boring stuff.

**Airpark Elements:** Lakes, golf cart search party, deer that distract Piper

---

### Episode 3: "The Shortcut"
> Sophia takes a shortcut across a neighbor's property to get home faster. She accidentally disturbs their vegetable garden and has to face them.

**Lesson:** Faster isn't always better. Other people's things matter.

**Airpark Elements:** Neighboring hangars, gardens, golf cart paths

---

### Episode 4: "Too Many Yeses"
> Sophia agrees to help Gus organize his hangar, help Mom at the fly-in pancake breakfast, AND play with Max — all at the same time. She can't keep any promises.

**Lesson:** Saying yes to everything means doing nothing well. It's okay to say "not right now."

**Airpark Elements:** Fly-in event, pancake breakfast, Gus's hangar

---

### Episode 5: "The Broken Prop"
> Sophia accidentally breaks a model airplane in Dad's workshop and hides it instead of telling him. The guilt follows her everywhere.

**Lesson:** Hiding a mistake makes it heavier. Truth is lighter, even when it's hard.

**Airpark Elements:** Dad's workshop, model airplanes, tools

---

### Episode 6: "Max Won't Jump"
> At the pool, Sophia pressures Max to jump off the diving board when he's scared. He does it to impress her and hurts himself. She feels terrible.

**Lesson:** Encouraging friends is good; pushing them past their limits isn't. Everyone's ready in their own time.

**Airpark Elements:** Swimming pool, summer day

---

### Episode 7: "The Ant Mountain"
> Sophia discovers a giant ant mound and decides to poke it with a stick to see what happens. She learns very quickly why that was a bad idea.

**Lesson:** Curiosity is great, but some things bite back. Watch before you touch.

**Airpark Elements:** Ant mounds, Texas wildlife, outdoor exploration

---

### Episode 8: "Gus's Long Story"
> Sophia is impatient with Gus's slow, rambling story about an old airplane. She leaves before the end. Later that day, she makes a mistake that his story would have warned her about.

**Lesson:** Patience isn't just waiting — it's staying open to what you might learn.

**Airpark Elements:** Gus's hangar, lawn chairs, "Choir Practice" reference (root beer for kids)

---

### Episode 9: "Lily Copies Everything"
> Little Lily starts copying everything Sophia does — including her bad habits. When Lily gets in trouble for something she learned from Sophia, it changes how Sophia sees herself.

**Lesson:** Younger kids learn from what you do, not what you say. You're already a role model.

**Airpark Elements:** Playground, multiple hangars, golf cart rides

---

### Episode 10: "The Invisible Danger" ⚠️ PROPELLER SAFETY
> Sophia almost walks in front of an airplane as the pilot starts the engine. Gus and Piper save her just in time. Dad teaches her the three rules of propeller safety, and she becomes an "Airpark Safety Expert."

**Lesson:** Some dangers you can't see — stay alert around airplanes with people inside. Propellers spin so fast they're invisible, and that's deadly.

**Airpark Elements:** Tie-down area, multiple aircraft, propeller safety demonstration, teaching younger kids

**The Three Rules:**
1. Stay behind the wing (propeller is in front)
2. Wait until they're gone (don't approach until plane has taxied away)
3. If you're not sure, ask (no such thing as a silly safety question)

---

### Episode 11: "First Flight" ✈️ SEASON FINALE
> Dad takes Sophia up in the Piper Cub for the first time. She's nervous but goes anyway. From the sky, she sees the whole airpark — and how all her season of adventures connect into one home.

**Lesson:** Being brave doesn't mean not being scared. It means going anyway — and the view from the other side is worth it.

**Airpark Elements:** Piper Cub, runway, aerial views of lakes/golf course/hangars, horses from above

**NOTE:** This episode works perfectly AFTER Episode 10 because Sophia has proven she understands airplane safety, making Dad confident to take her flying.

---

## SEASON ARC SUMMARY

Sophia begins the season jumping into everything without thinking. By the finale, she hasn't lost her curiosity — but she's learned to pause, ask questions, and trust the people who love her. The first flight represents her reward for growing: she's ready to see her world from a new perspective.

## PRODUCTION NOTES

**Consistent Visual Elements:**
- Sophia's outfit: Simple dress or shorts/t-shirt, always practical for adventure
- Piper always has a red collar
- Dad always wears baseball cap
- Gus always in lawn chair with coffee cup
- Yellow Piper Cub visible in background of home scenes

**Running Gags:**
- Piper's ears communicate his worry level
- Gus's stories always start "Did I ever tell you about the time..."
- Sophia's dramatic sighs when impatient
- Deer appearing at unexpected moments
