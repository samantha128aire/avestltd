## 1. INTRODUCTION

### Purpose
This manual establishes the training standards and procedures for all personnel involved in agricultural UAS operations for Avest Ltd. Completion of this training program is mandatory before any person may act as Pilot in Command (PIC) or crew member during agricultural operations.

### Scope
This training program covers:
- Regulatory requirements
- Aircraft systems and operation
- Agricultural application techniques
- Safety procedures
- Emergency response
- Chemical handling
- Weather evaluation
- Risk management

### Training Philosophy
Avest Ltd is committed to maintaining the highest standards of safety and professionalism. Our training program emphasizes:
- **Safety First**: All operations prioritize the safety of people, property, and the environment
- **Continuous Improvement**: Regular updates to reflect lessons learned and best practices
- **Proficiency**: Skills maintained through regular practice and evaluation
- **Accountability**: Every crew member responsible for safe operations

## 6. EMERGENCY PROCEDURES TRAINING

### Lost Link Procedures
1. **Aircraft Behavior**
   - Automatic hover for 3 seconds
   - Return-to-Home initiation
   - Altitude climb to RTH height
   - Direct flight to home point
   - Automatic landing at home point

2. **Pilot Actions**
   - Attempt to regain signal
   - Monitor aircraft status
   - Prepare for landing
   - Clear landing area
   - Call out aircraft position to crew

### Low Battery Procedures
1. **Level 1 Warning (30%)**
   - Complete current swath
   - Return to refill/recharge location
   - Land immediately

2. **Level 2 Warning (15%)**
   - Cease application
   - Return to nearest safe landing zone
   - Land immediately

3. **Critical Battery (10%)**
   - Automatic RTH may initiate
   - Aircraft may auto-land at current location
   - Do not attempt to override

### Motor Failure
- **Single Motor Failure:**
  - Aircraft will descend
  - Attempt controlled landing
  - Avoid people and property

- **Multiple Motor Failure:**
  - Rapid descent
  - Minimize impact damage
  - Evacuate area

### Crash/Incident Response
1. Ensure personal safety
2. Secure the area
3. Disconnect battery (if safe)
4. Document scene (photos, notes)
5. Notify authorities (if required)
6. Complete incident report
7. Preserve aircraft for investigation

## 8. COMPETENCY EVALUATION

### Knowledge Test
- 50-question written exam
- Passing score: 80%
- Topics covered:
  - Regulations
  - Aircraft systems
  - Weather
  - Agricultural operations
  - Safety procedures
  - Emergency response

### Practical Test
**Task 1: Pre-Flight Inspection**
- Complete checklist
- Identify discrepancies
- Make go/no-go decision

**Task 2: Mission Planning**
- Map field
- Set parameters
- Identify hazards
- Calculate chemical needs

**Task 3: Flight Operations**
- Execute spray mission
- Demonstrate precision
- Maintain VLOS
- Complete refill cycle

**Task 4: Emergency Procedures**
- Respond to simulated emergencies
- Demonstrate decision-making
- Execute proper procedures

**Task 5: Post-Flight**
- Clean aircraft
- Complete records
- Secure equipment
- Report status

### Evaluation Criteria
- **Satisfactory:** Performs all tasks safely and efficiently
- **Needs Improvement:** Completes tasks with minor errors
- **Unsatisfactory:** Cannot complete tasks safely

### Remedial Training
- Additional instruction as needed
- Re-evaluation required
- No operations until proficient
