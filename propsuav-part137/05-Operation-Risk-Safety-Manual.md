## 1. INTRODUCTION

### Purpose
This manual establishes the risk management and safety procedures for all agricultural UAS operations conducted by Avest Ltd. It provides a framework for identifying, assessing, and mitigating operational risks to ensure the safety of personnel, the public, property, and the environment.

### Scope
This manual applies to:
- All agricultural UAS operations
- All pilots and crew members
- All aircraft and equipment
- All operational locations

### Safety Philosophy
Avest Ltd is committed to:
- **Zero Harm**: No injuries to people, damage to property, or harm to the environment
- **Proactive Safety**: Identify and mitigate risks before incidents occur
- **Continuous Improvement**: Learn from experience and industry best practices
- **Open Communication**: Encourage reporting of hazards and near-misses
- **Compliance**: Meet or exceed all regulatory requirements

## 3. RISK MANAGEMENT PROCESS

### Five-Step Process

**Step 1: Identify Hazards**
- Review operations systematically
- Consider all phases of flight
- Include ground operations
- Consult with crew members
- Review past incidents

**Step 2: Assess Risks**
- Determine likelihood of occurrence
- Evaluate severity of consequences
- Calculate risk level (Likelihood × Severity)
- Prioritize risks

**Step 3: Analyze Risk Control Measures**
- Identify possible mitigation strategies
- Evaluate effectiveness of controls
- Consider cost and feasibility
- Select best control measures

**Step 4: Make Control Decisions**
- Implement selected controls
- Allocate resources
- Assign responsibilities
- Establish timelines
- Document decisions

**Step 5: Supervise and Review**
- Monitor effectiveness of controls
- Collect feedback from operations
- Adjust controls as needed
- Conduct periodic reviews
- Update risk assessments

---

### Risk Assessment Matrix

|  | **SEVERITY** |  |  |  |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **LIKELIHOOD** | Catastrophic | Critical | Moderate | Negligible |
| **Frequent** | Extremely High Risk | High Risk | Serious Risk | Medium Risk |
| **Likely** | High Risk | Serious Risk | Medium Risk | Low Risk |
| **Occasional** | Serious Risk | Medium Risk | Low Risk | Low Risk |
| **Seldom** | Medium Risk | Low Risk | Low Risk | Low Risk |
| **Unlikely** | Low Risk | Low Risk | Low Risk | Low Risk |

**Risk Acceptance Criteria:**
- **Extremely High/High:** Unacceptable - operations not authorized
- **Serious/Medium:** Acceptable with additional mitigation - requires Chief Supervisor approval
- **Low:** Acceptable - routine operations

## 4. OPERATIONAL HAZARDS

### 4.1 AIRCRAFT HAZARDS

#### Rotating Propellers
**Risk:** Severe injury or death from contact with spinning propellers

**Mitigation:**
- Maintain minimum 10-foot clearance during operation
- Never approach aircraft with motors running
- Shutdown motors before approaching
- Use visible barriers/cones during ground operations
- Brief all personnel on rotor hazards
- Post warning signs at operations site

#### Battery Fires
**Risk:** Thermal runaway leading to fire or explosion

**Mitigation:**
- Use only manufacturer-approved batteries
- Inspect batteries before each use
- Monitor battery temperature during operations
- Store batteries at 50-60% charge
- Use fireproof storage containers
- Maintain fire extinguisher nearby
- Never leave batteries charging unattended
- Dispose of damaged batteries properly

#### Structural Failure
**Risk:** In-flight breakup leading to uncontrolled crash

**Mitigation:**
- Conduct thorough pre-flight inspections
- Follow maintenance schedule
- Replace propellers per manufacturer guidelines
- Monitor for unusual vibrations or sounds
- Operate within aircraft limitations
- Avoid overstressing airframe

#### System Malfunctions
**Risk:** Loss of control or navigation

**Mitigation:**
- Maintain redundant safety systems
- Test all systems before flight
- Monitor telemetry during flight
- Establish return-to-home procedures
- Train for emergency scenarios
- Conduct regular firmware updates

---

### 4.2 ENVIRONMENTAL HAZARDS

#### Wind
**Risk:** Loss of control, chemical drift, collision with obstacles

**Mitigation:**
- Monitor wind speed and direction
- Establish operational wind limits (15 mph maximum)
- Adjust flight parameters for wind conditions
- Increase buffer zones in windy conditions
- Suspend operations if winds exceed limits
- Consider terrain effects on wind

#### Low Visibility
**Risk:** Collision with obstacles, loss of VLOS

**Mitigation:**
- Minimum visibility: 3 statute miles
- Suspend operations in fog, rain, or dust
- Maintain VLOS at all times
- Use high-visibility aircraft markings
- Position visual observers strategically

#### Darkness
**Risk:** Inability to maintain VLOS, reduced situational awareness

**Mitigation:**
- Daylight operations only
- Operations must cease at civil twilight
- Plan operations to complete before dusk
- Account for seasonal daylight variations

#### Temperature Extremes
**Risk:** Battery performance degradation, component failure

**Mitigation:**
- Monitor temperature forecasts
- Warm batteries before flight in cold weather
- Allow batteries to cool in hot weather
- Operate within temperature limits (-4°F to 113°F)
- Reduce payload in extreme temperatures

---

### 4.3 TERRAIN AND OBSTACLE HAZARDS

#### Power Lines
**Risk:** Collision causing crash, injury, and power outage

**Mitigation:**
- Conduct site survey before operations
- Mark power line locations on maps
- Brief all personnel on power line locations
- Maintain minimum 50-foot clearance
- Use obstacle avoidance systems
- Approach fields from safe directions

#### Trees and Vegetation
**Risk:** Collision, entanglement, chemical contamination

**Mitigation:**
- Map tree locations during survey
- Establish buffer zones
- Operate at appropriate altitude
- Avoid overhanging branches
- Plan escape routes
- Monitor wind effects on trajectory

#### Buildings and Structures
**Risk:** Collision, property damage, injury to occupants

**Mitigation:**
- Identify all structures before operations
- Establish 500-foot buffer zones
- No operations over occupied buildings
- Coordinate with property owners
- Plan flight paths away from structures

#### Vehicles and Equipment
**Risk:** Collision with moving vehicles or farm equipment

**Mitigation:**
- Coordinate with farm personnel
- Post warning signs and barriers
- Establish vehicle-free zones
- Maintain communications with ground crew
- Suspend operations if vehicles enter area

---

### 4.4 HUMAN FACTOR HAZARDS

#### Fatigue
**Risk:** Impaired judgment, delayed reactions, errors

**Mitigation:**
- Limit duty periods (8 hours flight ops maximum)
- Require adequate rest between operations
- Monitor pilot for signs of fatigue
- Encourage self-reporting
- Establish crew rest requirements

#### Distraction
**Risk:** Loss of situational awareness, procedural errors

**Mitigation:**
- Sterile cockpit procedures during critical phases
- Limit non-essential communications
- One pilot focus on flight, one on monitoring
- Use checklists to maintain focus
- Minimize external distractions

#### Complacency
**Risk:** Skipping procedures, normalizing deviations

**Mitigation:**
- Emphasize importance of all procedures
- Conduct surprise audits
- Review near-misses
- Rotate crew assignments
- Encourage questioning attitude

#### Pressure
**Risk:** Rushing, skipping safety steps, poor decisions

**Mitigation:**
- Management support for safety-first decisions
- Realistic scheduling
- Right to refuse unsafe operations
- No penalties for weather delays
- Adequate resources allocated

---

### 4.5 CHEMICAL HAZARDS

#### Exposure to Pesticides
**Risk:** Poisoning, chemical burns, long-term health effects

**Mitigation:**
- Use appropriate PPE for all chemical handling
- Follow product label instructions
- Maintain SDS sheets on-site
- Train personnel in chemical hazards
- Provide decontamination facilities
- Monitor for exposure symptoms
- Establish exposure response procedures

#### Chemical Spills
**Risk:** Environmental contamination, exposure, regulatory violations

**Mitigation:**
- Use secondary containment for storage
- Have spill kits readily available
- Train personnel in spill response
- Establish containment procedures
- Know reporting requirements
- Dispose of waste properly

#### Drift
**Risk:** Contamination of non-target areas, crops, water

**Mitigation:**
- Monitor wind conditions closely
- Establish buffer zones
- Use appropriate nozzles and droplet sizes
- Adjust application parameters
- Coordinate with adjacent landowners
- Document application conditions

---

### 4.6 OPERATIONAL HAZARDS

#### Airspace Violations
**Risk:** Enforcement action, collision with other aircraft

**Mitigation:**
- Verify airspace classification before operations
- Check NOTAMs and TFRs
- Maintain altitude limitations (200 ft AGL max)
- Class G airspace only
- Coordinate with local airports if nearby
- Monitor for other aircraft

#### Unauthorized Personnel in Operations Area
**Risk:** Injury from aircraft or chemical exposure

**Mitigation:**
- Establish perimeter boundaries
- Post warning signs
- Maintain visual observers
- Secure operations area
- Brief property owner
- Remove bystanders before operations

#### Equipment Failure
**Risk:** Incomplete application, crop damage, customer dissatisfaction

**Mitigation:**
- Maintain equipment per schedule
- Carry spare parts
- Have backup aircraft available
- Establish repair procedures
- Document all maintenance
- Test equipment before departure

## 5. MITIGATION STRATEGIES

### Hierarchy of Controls

**1. Elimination** (Most Effective)
- Remove the hazard entirely
- Example: Don't operate in high winds (eliminate wind hazard)

**2. Substitution**
- Replace with less hazardous alternative
- Example: Use less toxic chemicals when effective

**3. Engineering Controls**
- Physical modifications to reduce risk
- Example: Aircraft obstacle avoidance systems

**4. Administrative Controls**
- Policies, procedures, training
- Example: Operating limitations, checklists

**5. Personal Protective Equipment** (Least Effective)
- Last line of defense
- Example: Chemical-resistant gloves, safety glasses

## 6. SAFETY PROCEDURES

### 6.1 Pre-Operations Safety Briefing

Before each day's operations, conduct crew briefing covering:
1. Mission overview
2. Site-specific hazards
3. Weather conditions
4. Emergency procedures
5. Communication procedures
6. Individual responsibilities
7. Questions and concerns

### 6.2 Personal Protective Equipment

**Required PPE for Chemical Operations:**
- Chemical-resistant gloves
- Safety glasses or goggles
- Long-sleeved shirt and pants
- Closed-toe shoes
- Respirator (if required by product label)

**PPE Inspection:**
- Before each use
- Check for damage or deterioration
- Replace as needed
- Proper fit verification

### 6.3 Safe Operating Procedures

**Takeoff:**
1. Verify area clear
2. Brief crew on takeoff direction
3. Ensure all personnel clear of aircraft
4. Gradual throttle application
5. Monitor aircraft response
6. Establish stable hover before transition

**Landing:**
1. Select landing zone free of obstacles
2. Brief crew on approach path
3. Ensure area clear
4. Gradual descent
5. Soft touchdown
6. Reduce throttle smoothly
7. Disarm motors

**Chemical Loading:**
1. Don appropriate PPE
2. Mix chemicals per label
3. Filter mixture into tank
4. Secure tank cap
5. Prime spray system
6. Test spray pattern
7. Clean mixing equipment

## 7. EMERGENCY RESPONSE

### 7.1 Emergency Response Plan

**Emergency Contact Numbers:**
- Emergency Services: 911
- Poison Control: 1-800-222-1222
- Chief Supervisor: +1-508-922-9086
- Local Hospital: _______________
- FAA: _______________

### 7.2 Aircraft Emergency

**Uncontrolled Descent:**
1. Attempt to regain control
2. Activate RTH if functional
3. Clear personnel from impact zone
4. Call out aircraft position
5. Prepare for crash

**Post-Crash:**
1. Ensure personal safety
2. Disconnect battery (if safe to approach)
3. Secure area
4. Check for injuries
5. Check for chemical spill
6. Notify emergency services if needed
7. Preserve crash site
8. Document scene
9. Complete incident report

### 7.3 Chemical Emergency

**Personnel Exposure:**
1. Remove victim from contaminated area
2. Remove contaminated clothing
3. Flush affected area with water (15+ minutes)
4. Call Poison Control
5. Seek medical attention
6. Bring SDS sheet to hospital
7. Document exposure

**Chemical Spill:**
1. Stop source if safe
2. Evacuate unnecessary personnel
3. Don appropriate PPE
4. Contain spill with absorbent materials
5. Prevent entry to water sources
6. Clean up spill per product label
7. Dispose of waste properly
8. Report spill if required
9. Document incident

### 7.4 Medical Emergency

**Injury Response:**
1. Assess situation safety
2. Call 911 if serious
3. Provide first aid if trained
4. Do not move victim unless necessary
5. Secure operations area
6. Preserve scene if needed for investigation
7. Complete injury report

- All incidents reported to Chief Supervisor within 24 hours

- Findings shared with all personnel
