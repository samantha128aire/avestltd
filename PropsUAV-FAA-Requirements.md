## Overview
Operating agricultural spray drones commercially requires TWO major certifications:
1. **Part 137 Agricultural Aircraft Operator Certificate (AAOC)**
2. **FAA Exemption** (for relief from specific regulations)

**PLUS** additional requirements for drones over 55 pounds.

## Part 137 Agricultural Aircraft Operator Certificate (AAOC)

### What is Part 137?
14 CFR Part 137 governs the use of aircraft (including drones) to dispense or spray substances for agricultural purposes:
- Economic poisons (pesticides, herbicides, fungicides)
- Plant nourishment substances
- Soil treatments
- Pest control
- Disinfectants (considered "economic poisons" by FAA)

### Prerequisites
✅ **You must have FIRST:**
1. FAA Part 107 Remote Pilot Certificate (you already have this - Remote Pilot UAS #3932937)
2. Approved FAA Exemption (see process below)

### Application Process
**Timeline:** Can begin AFTER receiving exemption approval

**Steps:**
1. Complete FAA Form 8710-3
2. Include your exemption number on the form
3. Submit to: UAS137Certificates@faa.gov

**Required Information:**
- Full legal name and physical address (no P.O. boxes)
- Chief Supervisor of Operations (name, phone, email, address)
- Pilot in Command name and certificate number (your Remote Pilot #3932937)
- Aircraft details (make, model, registration number)
- Exemption number (from Step 1)

**Important:** The name on Form 8710-3 must exactly match the exemption holder name.

## FAA Exemption Process

### Timeline
⏰ **CRITICAL:** Must submit petition at least **120 days before** you need to start operations

### What You're Asking Exemption From
You need relief from these specific regulations (standard for ag drone operations):
- §§ 61.3(a)(1)(i), 91.7(a), 91.119(c), 91.121, 91.151(b)
- §§ 91.405(a), 91.407(a)(1), 91.409(a)(1), 91.409(a)(2)
- §§ 91.417(a), 91.417(b), 91.403(b)
- §§ 137.19(c), 137.19(d), 137.19(e)(2)(ii), 137.19(e)(2)(iii), 137.19(e)(2)(v)
- §§ 137.31(a), 137.31(b), 137.33(a), 137.33(b), 137.41(c), 137.42

### Application Steps

**Step 1: Prepare Documentation**

You'll need to create several manuals/documents:
1. **Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)**
2. **Pre-Flight Checklist** (confidential)
3. **Maintenance Log** (confidential)
4. **Aircrew Training Manual** (confidential)
5. **Operation Risk and Safety Manual** (confidential)
6. **Flight Operations and Procedures Manual** (confidential)
7. **FAA Part 107 Training Curriculum** (if training others)
8. **Petition Summary** explaining your request

Plus manufacturer documentation:
- User manuals
- Safety guidelines
- Battery user guides
- Charger guides

💡 **Good news:** You can view and copy from existing approved exemptions at:
https://www.regulations.gov/search?filter=137.41(c)

**Step 2: Submit Petition**

Submit online at: https://www.regulations.gov/commenton/FAA-2007-0001-0001

Your petition must include:
- Your name and mailing address (contact info optional but recommended)
- Specific CFR sections you're seeking exemption from (see list above)
- Extent of relief sought and reasons why
- Why granting exemption serves public interest
- Why exemption won't adversely affect safety
- Summary for Federal Register publication
- All supporting documents

**Mark documents as "CONFIDENTIAL" if you don't want them publicly visible**

**Step 3: Provide Additional Information**

The FAA will likely send a Request for Information (RFI) asking for:
- Full legal name and physical address (no P.O. boxes)
- Chief Supervisor of Operations contact details
- Pilot in Command name and certificate number
- Aircraft make, model, and registration number (FA# or N#)
- Owner name for each aircraft

**Step 4: Wait**

⏰ Processing time: **3 weeks to 120 days**
- FAA may contact you with questions
- Be patient - they rarely provide status updates
- You'll receive email decision from: 9-AVS-ARM320-Exemptions@FAA.gov

**Step 5: Receive Exemption, Then Apply for Part 137**

Once exemption is approved, complete Form 8710-3 for your AAOC.

## Drones Over 55 Pounds - Special Requirements

### Registration Differences

**Under 55 lbs:**
- Register with FAA UAS registry
- Receive "FA" registration number (e.g., FA3######)

**Over 55 lbs:**
- Must register as "aircraft" (not just UAS)
- Receive "N" registration number (e.g., N123UA)
- More stringent requirements

### Additional Exemptions Needed

For drones **over 55 pounds**, you need exemptions from additional regulations:
- **Part 61** (pilot certification requirements)
- **Part 91** (general operating rules)
- **Section 44807** (for heavy drones)

### Airworthiness

Drones over 55 lbs may require:
- Special airworthiness certificate
- More detailed maintenance documentation
- Enhanced operational procedures

### Certificate of Waiver or Authorization (COA)

⚠️ **Important:** Some operations may also require a COA from ATO (Air Traffic Organization) for specific airspace operations.
