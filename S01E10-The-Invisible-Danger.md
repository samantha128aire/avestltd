# Sophia's Airpark Adventures
## Season 1, Episode 10: "The Invisible Danger"

**Runtime:** 10-12 minutes  
**Target Age:** 3-6 years  
**Theme:** Some dangers you can't see — stay alert around airplanes with people inside

---

## CHARACTERS

- **SOPHIA** (6) — Curious, energetic, learning to think before acting
- **PIPER** — Sophia's loyal tan mutt
- **MOM (ANNA)** — Warm Filipina mother
- **DAD (BILL)** — Calm pilot, wears baseball cap
- **GUS** — Retired pilot, 70s, white beard
- **MR. JENKINS** — Neighbor pilot with a blue and white Cessna 172

---

## COLD OPEN

### SCENE 1: MORNING AT THE RUNWAY
**Location:** Near the runway, watching planes  
**Time:** Early morning, golden light

**VISUAL:** Sophia and Piper sit on the grass near the runway. A Cessna 182 taxis past, propeller spinning. The wind from it ruffles Sophia's hair.

*SOPHIA*  
*(to Piper, excited)*  
Look at them go! One day that'll be us, Piper!

**VISUAL:** The plane lifts off gracefully. Sophia waves.

*SOPHIA (V.O.)*  
*(narrating)*  
Living at an airpark means I get to see airplanes every day. But Dad says airplanes are like sleeping dragons — beautiful... but you have to know how to be safe around them.

**VISUAL:** Title card appears over wide shot of the airpark.

---

## ACT ONE: THE CLOSE CALL

### SCENE 2: HELPING GUS
**Location:** Gus's hangar  
**Time:** Mid-morning

**VISUAL:** Gus's hangar is open. His red and white Piper Cherokee sits inside. Sophia is helping him organize tools while Piper naps in the shade.

*GUS*  
*(handing her a rag)*  
Sophia, can you wipe down that propeller blade for me? Keeps the bugs off.

*SOPHIA*  
Sure!

**VISUAL:** Sophia carefully wipes the propeller blade. It's still, stationary, safe.

*SOPHIA*  
Gus? How fast does the propeller spin when the engine is on?

*GUS*  
*(chuckling)*  
Oh, about two thousand times a minute. So fast you can't even see it.

*SOPHIA*  
*(eyes wide)*  
Really? That fast?

*GUS*  
Yep. That's why you NEVER walk in front of an airplane if someone's inside it. Even if the propeller's not moving yet.

*SOPHIA*  
Why not?

*GUS*  
Because it might start any second. And once it does... 
*(makes a whooshing sound)*  
...you can't see the blades anymore. They're invisible.

*SOPHIA*  
*(thoughtful)*  
Invisible blades sound scary.

*GUS*  
They are. But if you remember the rule, you'll always be safe.

---

### SCENE 3: PLAYING NEAR THE TIE-DOWNS
**Location:** Visitor tie-down area  
**Time:** Late morning

**VISUAL:** Sophia and Piper are playing fetch near the tie-down area. Several airplanes are parked: a Cessna 172, a Piper Warrior, a Kitfox.

*SOPHIA*  
*(throwing a ball)*  
Go get it, Piper!

**VISUAL:** The ball bounces... and rolls UNDER a blue and white Cessna 172. Piper stops, looking at Sophia.

*SOPHIA*  
*(laughing)*  
Oops! It's okay, I'll get it.

**VISUAL:** She starts to walk around the front of the airplane toward the ball.

**SOUND:** A door opens. MR. JENKINS climbs into the Cessna's pilot seat.

**VISUAL:** Sophia doesn't notice. She's focused on the ball under the plane.

*SOPHIA*  
Almost got it...

**SOUND:** The airplane's engine COUGHS. About to start.

**VISUAL:** Piper's ears shoot up. He BARKS urgently.

*PIPER*  
*(BARK! BARK! BARK!)*

*SOPHIA*  
*(looking back, confused)*  
Piper, what's wrong?

**VISUAL:** GUS appears from behind his hangar, moving FAST for an old man.

*GUS*  
*(loud, urgent)*  
SOPHIA! STOP! GET BACK!

**VISUAL:** Sophia freezes. Gus reaches her, gently but firmly pulling her back by the shoulder.

**SOUND:** The engine ROARS to life. The propeller spins — a blur.

**VISUAL:** Sophia watches, stunned, as the propeller becomes invisible. She realizes she was just about to walk right in front of it.

*SOPHIA*  
*(whisper)*  
Oh...

---

## ACT TWO: THE LESSON

### SCENE 4: AFTER THE CLOSE CALL
**Location:** Gus's hangar, lawn chairs  
**Time:** Late morning

**VISUAL:** Sophia sits in a lawn chair, shaken. Piper lies at her feet. Gus sits beside her, calm but serious.

*GUS*  
You okay, kiddo?

*SOPHIA*  
*(quietly)*  
I almost walked in front of it.

*GUS*  
But you didn't. Because Piper warned you. And I was watching.

*SOPHIA*  
*(tears forming)*  
But what if you weren't there?

*GUS*  
*(gently)*  
That's why we have the rule. You don't walk in front of an airplane if someone's inside it. Ever. Even if the engine's not on.

*SOPHIA*  
Because it might start?

*GUS*  
Exactly. Mr. Jenkins didn't know you were there. He probably looked around and yelled "clear prop" like he's supposed to. But you're small, Sophia. Hard to see from up in that cockpit. Even when pilots do everything right, they might not see little people. That's why you stay clear. Always.

**VISUAL:** Gus doesn't finish the sentence about what could have happened. He doesn't need to. Sophia understands.

*SOPHIA*  
I didn't even see him get in.

*GUS*  
That's the thing about danger, Sophia. Sometimes it sneaks up on you. That's why we stay alert.

**VISUAL:** Sophia looks at Piper.

*SOPHIA*  
Piper knew.

*GUS*  
*(smiling)*  
Dogs are smart like that. He's a good co-pilot.

**VISUAL:** Sophia hugs Piper tight.

---

### SCENE 5: DAD'S LESSON
**Location:** Sophia's house — back porch  
**Time:** Afternoon

**VISUAL:** Bill is working on a small engine on the porch. Sophia sits nearby, still quiet. Anna brings them lemonade.

*ANNA*  
Gus told me what happened.

*SOPHIA*  
*(looking down)*  
I wasn't thinking.

*BILL*  
*(wiping his hands, sitting beside her)*  
Sophia, I want to show you something.

**VISUAL:** He picks up a small handheld fan. Turns it on. The blades blur.

*BILL*  
Can you see the blades?

*SOPHIA*  
*(shaking her head)*  
Not really. They're too fast.

*BILL*  
But they're still there, right?

*SOPHIA*  
Yes.

*BILL*  
Now imagine those blades are as big as you are. And made of metal. And spinning two thousand times a minute.

**VISUAL:** Sophia's eyes widen.

*SOPHIA*  
That's... that's really dangerous.

*BILL*  
It is. But it's also how airplanes fly. The propeller isn't bad — it's powerful. And powerful things need respect.

*SOPHIA*  
But... don't pilots look around first?

*BILL*  
*(nodding)*  
Good question. Yes, we're supposed to yell "clear prop!" and look all around before starting the engine. Mr. Jenkins did that today.

*SOPHIA*  
Then why didn't he see me?

*BILL*  
*(gently)*  
Because you're small. And you were low to the ground. Even when pilots do everything right, they might not see little people near the plane. That's why YOU have to stay clear.

**VISUAL:** Sophia understands — she can't rely on pilots seeing her.

*SOPHIA*  
So it's my job to stay safe?

*BILL*  
Exactly. You're responsible for you.

*SOPHIA*  
How do I stay safe?

*BILL*  
*(turning off the fan)*  
Three rules. Want to learn them?

*SOPHIA*  
*(nodding)*  
Yes.

---

### SCENE 6: THE THREE RULES
**Location:** Airpark — various locations (montage)  
**Time:** Afternoon

**VISUAL:** Bill walks Sophia through the airpark, demonstrating the rules. Anna and Piper follow.

**RULE 1: STAY BEHIND THE WING**

**VISUAL:** Bill stands beside his yellow Piper Cub. He points to the wing.

*BILL*  
If someone's in an airplane, always stay behind the wing. The propeller is in front. The wing protects you.

**VISUAL:** Sophia practices walking around the plane from behind.

*SOPHIA*  
Behind the wing. Got it.

---

**RULE 2: WAIT UNTIL THEY'RE GONE**

**VISUAL:** At the tie-downs. A Cessna 150 has someone inside doing a preflight check.

*BILL*  
See that pilot? He's getting ready. We don't know when he'll start the engine. So what do we do?

*SOPHIA*  
*(thinking)*  
We wait?

*BILL*  
Exactly. We wait until he's taxied away. Then the propeller can't hurt us.

**VISUAL:** They watch from a safe distance as the Cessna starts, taxis, and departs.

*SOPHIA*  
Now it's safe!

*BILL*  
Now it's safe.

---

**RULE 3: IF YOU'RE NOT SURE, ASK**

**VISUAL:** Back at Gus's hangar. Gus is sitting in his lawn chair.

*BILL*  
Sophia, what if you see an airplane and you're not sure if it's safe to get close?

*SOPHIA*  
*(looking at Gus)*  
I ask?

*GUS*  
*(grinning)*  
That's my girl! I'd rather answer a hundred questions than see someone get hurt.

*SOPHIA*  
Even if it's a silly question?

*GUS*  
Especially if it's a silly question. There's no such thing as a silly safety question.

---

## ACT THREE: PROVING SHE LEARNED

### SCENE 7: THE NEXT MORNING
**Location:** Airpark — runway area  
**Time:** Morning

**VISUAL:** Sophia and Piper are playing fetch again. The ball rolls near a parked Kitfox. Sophia runs after it... then stops.

**VISUAL:** She looks carefully. No one inside. She checks the cockpit windows.

*SOPHIA*  
*(to Piper)*  
No one's in there. It's safe.

**VISUAL:** She retrieves the ball. As she walks back, a pilot approaches the Kitfox with a flight bag.

*SOPHIA*  
*(calling out)*  
Excuse me! Are you about to fly?

*PILOT*  
*(surprised, smiling)*  
I am! In just a minute.

*SOPHIA*  
Okay! We'll wait over here until you're gone!

**VISUAL:** Sophia and Piper move to a safe spot behind the wing line. The pilot waves, impressed.

*PILOT*  
Smart kid!

---

### SCENE 8: GUS'S APPROVAL
**Location:** Gus's hangar  
**Time:** Late morning

**VISUAL:** Gus is polishing his propeller. Sophia approaches with Piper.

*SOPHIA*  
Gus! I remembered the rules today!

*GUS*  
*(looking up)*  
That so?

*SOPHIA*  
Yep! A pilot was getting in his plane, so Piper and I waited until he flew away. THEN we got our ball.

*GUS*  
*(standing, proud)*  
Now THAT's what I like to hear. You know what that makes you?

*SOPHIA*  
What?

*GUS*  
An official Airpark Safety Expert.

**VISUAL:** Sophia beams.

*SOPHIA*  
Really?

*GUS*  
Really. And you know what experts do?

*SOPHIA*  
What?

*GUS*  
They teach other people. So if you ever see a kid who doesn't know the rules...

*SOPHIA*  
*(excited)*  
I can teach them!

*GUS*  
You got it.

---

### SCENE 9: TEACHING LILY
**Location:** Playground near the hangars  
**Time:** Afternoon

**VISUAL:** LILY (4 years old) is at the playground with her mom. Sophia runs over with Piper.

*SOPHIA*  
Hi Lily!

*LILY*  
*(brightening)*  
Sophia! Want to play?

*SOPHIA*  
Yes! But first I want to teach you something really important.

**VISUAL:** Sophia kneels to Lily's level, serious but kind.

*SOPHIA*  
If you see an airplane and someone's inside it, you have to stay behind the wing. And you have to wait until they fly away. Because the propeller is invisible when it spins, and it can hurt you.

*LILY*  
*(wide-eyed)*  
Invisible?

*SOPHIA*  
Yep. But if you remember the rule, you'll always be safe. Just like me and Piper.

**VISUAL:** Lily looks at Piper, then back at Sophia.

*LILY*  
Okay! I'll remember!

*SOPHIA*  
*(grinning)*  
Good! Now let's play!

---

### SCENE 10: REFLECTION
**Location:** Sophia's bedroom  
**Time:** Evening

**VISUAL:** Sophia is in bed. Anna sits beside her, brushing her hair gently.

*ANNA*  
I'm proud of you, anak.

*SOPHIA*  
For what?

*ANNA*  
For learning the rules. For teaching Lily. For listening when Gus and Dad taught you.

*SOPHIA*  
*(thoughtful)*  
Mom? I was really scared when I realized I almost walked in front of that propeller.

*ANNA*  
I know. But you know what?

*SOPHIA*  
What?

*ANNA*  
Being scared is okay. It means you understand. And understanding means you'll be careful.

**VISUAL:** Sophia nods, hugging Piper.

*SOPHIA*  
I love airplanes. But I'm going to be smart around them now.

*ANNA*  
That's all we want.

---

### SCENE 11: CLOSING
**Location:** Airpark (montage)  
**Time:** Next morning

**VISUAL:** Sophia and Piper walk through the airpark confidently. 

*SOPHIA (V.O.)*  
*(narrating)*  
Today I learned that some dangers are invisible. But that doesn't mean you can't be safe.

**VISUAL:** She waves at Mr. Jenkins, who's doing a preflight check on his Cessna. He waves back.

*SOPHIA (V.O.)*  
You just have to know the rules. And remember them. Always.

**VISUAL:** A plane takes off in the background. Sophia and Piper watch from a safe distance, smiling.

*SOPHIA (V.O.)*  
Stay behind the wing. Wait until they're gone. And if you're not sure... ask.

**VISUAL:** Wide shot of the airpark — golden morning light, Sophia and Piper heading home, airplanes gleaming in the sun.

*SOPHIA (V.O.)*  
That's how you stay safe. That's how you stay smart. And that's how you get to keep exploring.

# PRODUCTION NOTES

**Key Animation Moments:**
- Propeller starting: blur effect showing it become "invisible"
- Close-up of Sophia's face when she realizes the danger
- Piper's urgent barking (ears forward, alert stance)
- Dad's fan demonstration (clear visual of blades blurring)
- Sophia confidently walking around planes correctly

**Sound Design:**
- Engine cough before starting (builds tension)
- Propeller sound: low rumble to high whine
- Piper's urgent bark (distinct from playful barks)
- Wind from propeller wash

**Music Cues:**
- Tension build as Sophia approaches plane
- Relief/safe feeling when Gus pulls her back
- Educational, upbeat during rule-learning montage
- Proud, confident theme when she teaches Lily

**Safety Message Emphasis:**
- Clear, simple rules repeated multiple times
- Visual reinforcement: always show safe behavior after learning
- Adult supervision present in all examples
- Positive role models (Gus, Dad, Mr. Jenkins)

## EPISODE PLACEMENT

This episode comes as **NEW Episode 10**, pushing "First Flight" to **Episode 11** (Season Finale).

**Why this placement:**
- Sophia needs this safety knowledge BEFORE flying with Dad
- Shows her growth: she's learned to respect rules and teach others
- Dad will feel confident taking her flying because she's proven she understands airplane safety
- Propeller awareness is foundational for anyone living at an airpark
