**Safety Message of This Episode:**  
When adults who love you make rules, they're not trying to ruin your fun — they're protecting you from dangers you might not see yet. Rules come from experience and love.

**How It's Modeled:**
1. Sophia ignores multiple warnings (Mom, Gus)
2. Consequence happens (gets stuck, scared, hurt)
3. Adults rescue with love (not anger)
4. Dad teaches with patience and questions (not lectures)
5. Sophia reflects and understands WHY the rule existed
6. Next morning, Sophia demonstrates she learned (stops herself from approaching gray hangar again)

**Positive Role Models:**
- **Anna:** Sets clear boundaries, follows up with warmth and forgiveness
- **Bill:** Asks questions, explains reasoning, teaches without shaming
- **Gus:** Gives warnings with wisdom and patience, teases gently the next day (models that mistakes don't define you)
- **Piper:** Loyal, tries to warn Sophia, gets help when she's in trouble

**What Kids Learn:**
- Curiosity is good, but safety matters
- Adults make rules to protect you, not to be mean
- When you make a mistake, you can ask for help
- Saying "I'm sorry" and meaning it is important
- People who love you forgive you
- You can learn from mistakes without being defined by them
