SOPHIA'S AIRPARK ADVENTURES - Episode 1: "The New Hangar" (Pilot)

TARGET: Ages 3-6 | RUNTIME: 10-12 minutes | STYLE: Warm Disney/Pixar-inspired family animation | THEME: Rules exist for reasons you might not see yet

SETTING: Hilltop Lakes Airpark, Central Texas. Grass runway (NOT paved), metal hangars (tan/white/green), yellow house, big open sky, live oak trees, lakes in background. Golden morning light, clear sunny day.

MAIN CHARACTERS:
• SOPHIA (6): Filipina girl, long dark hair, round face, big brown eyes, warm tan skin, red dress, curious and impulsive
• PIPER: Tan mutt, red collar, floppy ears (UP=happy, BACK=worried), loyal companion
• ANNA (Mom): Filipina, 40s, ponytail, blue blouse, warm and patient, calls Sophia "anak"
• BILL (Dad): Late 40s, short beard, ALWAYS wears navy baseball cap, gray t-shirt, calm teacher
• GUS: Retired pilot, 70s, white beard, plaid shirt, sits in lawn chair with coffee

AIRCRAFT: Yellow Piper J-3 Cub (Dad's plane), shiny Beechcraft Bonanza (visitor), blue/white Cessna 172, red/white Piper Cherokee (Gus's). Background: Cessna 182, Kitfox. Old airplane frame inside gray hangar. NO JETS. NO MILITARY AIRCRAFT.

STORY:
Morning: Sophia wants to see new airplane that landed. Mom warns: "Stay away from old gray hangar—it's not safe." Sophia barely listens. She explores airpark with Piper, meets Gus who also warns about dangerous gray hangar. She finds shiny Bonanza at tie-downs but becomes curious about mysterious gray hangar.

Midday: Despite warnings, Sophia peeks inside gray hangar. Sees beautiful old airplane frame, dusty and abandoned. Steps inside "just to look." Wind blows doors partially closed. She steps on rotted floorboard—CRACK—foot goes through, gets stuck. Scared, alone, crying. Piper barks frantically outside then squeezes through, stays with her.

Afternoon: Dad and Mom find her (Piper led them). Dad carefully frees her foot. Ankle bandaged, scared but okay. On porch, Dad teaches: "Rules aren't just 'no'—they're shortcuts. Someone learned the hard way so you don't have to." Sophia realizes adults knew danger she couldn't see.

Evening: Mom tucks her in. Sophia apologizes, Mom forgives. Learns Piper saved her by getting help.

Next morning: Sophia explores again but STOPS at Gus's hangar when she sees gray building. Pauses, thinks, turns away. Shows she learned lesson.

LESSON: When people who love you make rules, they're protecting you from dangers you might not see. Curiosity is good, but listening matters.

TONE: Warm, educational, gentle consequence without trauma. Sophia makes mistake but learns through love and reflection.

VISUAL STYLE: Natural Texas landscape, warm golden lighting, expressive characters, big open skies, grass runway, metal hangars, inviting community feel.
